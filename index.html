Understanding these threats is your first line of defense against cybercriminals.

Essential Safety Tips

  • Use Strong Passwords: Create unique passwords with a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols. Use a password manager to keep track of them.
  • Enable Two-Factor Authentication: Add an extra layer of security to your accounts by requiring a second form of verification.
  • Think Before You Click: Don't click on suspicious links in emails or messages. Verify the sender's identity first.
  • Keep Software Updated: Regular updates patch security vulnerabilities that criminals could exploit.
  • Secure Your Wi-Fi: Use a strong password for your home network and avoid public Wi-Fi for sensitive transactions.
  • Backup Your Data: Regularly backup important files to an external drive or cloud service to protect against ransomware.
  • Verify Before Sharing: Never share personal or financial information unless you've verified the recipient's legitimacy.

Report Cybercrime

If you've been a victim of cybercrime, it's important to report it immediately.

Emergency Contacts

National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal (India):
Visit: cybercrime.gov.in
Helpline: 1930

FBI Internet Crime Complaint Center (USA):
Visit: ic3.gov

Action Fraud (UK):
Call: 0300 123 2040

Steps to Take:
1. Document everything (screenshots, emails, messages)
2. Report to local authorities and cybercrime units
3. Change passwords immediately
4. Monitor your financial accounts
5. Inform your bank if financial fraud is involved
